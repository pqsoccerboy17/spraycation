# Funding Strategy -- SPRAYCATION 2026

## Overview
SPRAYCATION 2026 aims to scale from 5 cities (2025) to 8-10 cities nationwide. This requires a diversified funding approach combining corporate sponsorship, arts grants, wall partner revenue, and in-kind support.

## Revenue Targets (Estimated)
Based on 2025 pricing tiers and 8-10 city target:

| Revenue Source | Per City | 8 Cities | 10 Cities |
|---------------|----------|----------|-----------|
| Magic Maker ($25K) | $25,000 | $200,000 | $250,000 |
| Culture Partner ($15K) | $15,000 | $120,000 | $150,000 |
| Community Ally ($5K) | $5,000 | $40,000 | $50,000 |

Target: $200K-$450K total 2026 revenue depending on mix and scale.

## Funding Channels

### 1. Corporate Sponsors (PRIMARY)
National brands that want authentic community activation, content, and cultural alignment. Not traditional advertising -- this is purpose-driven marketing.

**Ideal Sponsor Profile:**
- Values community, creativity, women's empowerment
- Wants authentic content (not staged ads)
- Needs multi-city brand awareness
- Cares about cultural impact, not just impressions
- Budget: $5K-$25K+ per partnership (Community Ally to Magic Maker tiers)

**Approach:** Lead with the Denver case study (3x foot traffic, Westword press, "more buzz than 3 years of marketing"). Show pricing tiers. Emphasize evergreen content and permanent exposure vs. traditional ad spend.

### 2. Arts Grants (SECONDARY)
Public art, community development, and arts funding from government and foundation sources.

**Types:**
- National Endowment for the Arts (NEA)
- State arts councils (per target city/state)
- Municipal public art programs
- Community development grants
- Women in arts grants
- Travel/touring artist grants

**Approach:** Apply 6-12 months ahead. Frame SPRAYCATION as community development + public art + women-led creative enterprise.

### 3. Wall Partner Revenue (PROVEN)
Local businesses partner at the Community Ally ($5K) or Culture Partner ($15K) level to host a mural. This is already working -- Denver and Bozeman both had paying wall partners in 2025.

### 4. In-Kind Partnerships
Brands that provide products/services instead of cash:
- Paint/supplies (most critical)
- Vehicle/fuel sponsorship for AquaBurn
- Food and beverage for events and tour travel
- Apparel/merch
- Accommodation (hotel partnerships)
- Equipment (cameras, lighting)

### 5. Crowdfunding / Community Support
- Venmo (@muraltour) for individual contributions
- Potential Kickstarter/GoFundMe for 2026 tour
- Fiscal sponsorship through a 501(c)(3) could make donations tax-deductible
